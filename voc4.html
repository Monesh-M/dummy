Accounting, Banking, Investment, and Finance Terms

1. Accounting Terms

  • Assets: Resources owned by a company that have economic value (e.g., cash, property, equipment).
  • Liabilities: Financial obligations or debts that a company owes (e.g., loans, accounts payable).
  • Equity: The owner's interest in the company, calculated as Assets - Liabilities.
  • Revenue: The total income generated from selling goods or services.
  • Expenses: Costs incurred during the process of earning revenue (e.g., rent, salaries, utilities).
  • Profit (Net Income): The financial gain, calculated as Revenue - Expenses.

2. Banking Terms

  • Interest Rate: The percentage charged by a lender for borrowing money or earned on deposits.
  • Loan: A sum of money borrowed that is expected to be paid back with interest.
  • Mortgage: A type of loan used to purchase real estate, where the property itself serves as collateral.
  • Credit Score: A numerical rating that represents an individual's creditworthiness.
  • Collateral: An asset pledged as security for a loan.

3. Investment Terms

  • Stock: A type of security that represents ownership in a company.
  • Bond: A fixed-income investment where an investor loans money to an entity (government or corporate) that borrows the funds for a defined period at a fixed interest rate.
  • Dividend: A portion of a company's earnings paid to shareholders.
  • Capital Gain: The profit earned from the sale of an asset, such as stocks, bonds, or real estate.
  • Mutual Fund: A pool of funds collected from many investors to invest in securities such as stocks, bonds, and other assets.
  • Exchange-Traded Fund (ETF): A type of investment fund traded on stock exchanges, similar to a mutual fund but can be bought and sold throughout the day.

4. Economic Terms

  • Gross Domestic Product (GDP): The total value of all goods and services produced within a country in a specific period.
  • Inflation: The rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services rises, reducing purchasing power.
  • Deflation: A decrease in the general price level of goods and services.
  • Recession: A period of temporary economic decline, typically identified by a fall in GDP for two consecutive quarters.
  • Supply and Demand: The basic economic principle where the price of a good is determined by the relationship between its availability (supply) and the desire of people to buy it (demand).

5. Business Strategy Terms

  • Business Plan: A document that outlines a company's goals, strategies, and financial projections.
  • Market Research: The process of gathering information about a target market, including customer needs, preferences, and behavior.
  • Competitive Advantage: A condition that allows a company to produce goods or services better or more cheaply than its competitors.
  • Branding: The process of creating a unique identity and image for a product or company in the consumer's mind.
  • Supply Chain Management: The management of the flow of goods and services, including all processes that transform raw materials into final products.

6. Corporate Finance Terms

  • Capital Structure: The mix of debt and equity that a company uses to finance its operations.
  • Leverage: The use of borrowed funds to increase the potential return on investment.
  • Initial Public Offering (IPO): The first time a company offers its shares to the public.
  • Merger and Acquisition (M&A): The process of consolidating companies, where a merger is a combination of two companies, and an acquisition is one company taking over another.
  • Working Capital: The difference between a company’s current assets and current liabilities, showing its ability to meet short-term obligations.

7. Taxation Terms

  • Income Tax: A tax levied by the government directly on income, especially an annual tax on personal income.
  • Corporate Tax: A tax imposed on the net income of a company.
  • Value-Added Tax (VAT): A consumption tax levied on the value added to goods and services at each stage of production or distribution.
  • Tax Deduction: An expense that can be subtracted from taxable income to reduce the amount of tax owed.
  • Tax Evasion: The illegal practice of not paying taxes owed.
